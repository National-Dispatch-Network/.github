# National Dispatch Network

National Dispatch Network operates a proprietary B2B meteorological routing utility. Our systems are designed to synthesize continuous environmental telemetry with localized structural density data, facilitating rapid emergency response logistics across North America and Australia.

## System Architecture Overview

Our infrastructure is built to process real-time environmental stress factors and automate the deployment of mitigation resources. The core logic remains closed-source to protect our proprietary sector-mapping algorithms, but operates on three fundamental pillars:

* **Telemetry Ingestion:** Continuous monitoring of localized environmental thresholds, including wind shear loading, surface inundation, and hail impact metrics.
* **Actuarial Grid Mapping:** Cross-referencing active weather anomalies with residential housing density and median structural age to predict localized hazard volumes.
* **Automated Sector Dispatch:** Dynamic pairing of high-urgency property threats with verified, tier-1 rapid-response networks based on geographic proximity and compliance standing.

## Active Routing Endpoints

Our central telemetry engine currently routes localized weather data to the following sector endpoints:

* [National Storm Dispatch](https://nationalstormdispatch.com) - Structural Storm Damage Routing
* [National Tree Dispatch](https://nationaltreedispatch.com) - Arboricultural Threat Dispatch
* [National Water Dispatch](https://nationalwaterdispatch.com) - Inundation & Water Extraction Routing

## Network Compliance & Governance

To maintain grid integrity and consumer safety, all destination endpoints receiving automated payloads from our network are strictly bound by localized compliance protocols. Routing eligibility requires continuous maintenance of state/provincial licensure, recognized commercial liability insurance, and adherence to established mitigation baselines (HAAG, ISA TRAQ, IICRC).
